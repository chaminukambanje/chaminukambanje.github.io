1. The Document Flow

Sales Cycle

  1. Sales Quote: No impact on G/L or Inventory. Can be archived.
  2. Sales Order: Increases "Committed" inventory. Required for partial shipments.
  3. Sales Shipment: The warehouse step. Moves inventory out (Credit Inventory, Debit COGS).
  4. Sales Invoice: The financial step. Updates Customer Ledger (Debit AR, Credit Sales).

Purchasing Cycle

Similar to sales, but with "Receipts" instead of Shipments.

Qty. to Receive: If you order 10 items, but the vendor only sends 8, set "Qty. to Receive" to 8 and post. The PO remains open with 2 items on "Outstanding Quantity".

2. General Journals

Used for transactions that don't involve a standard document (like adjusting bad debt or monthly accruals).

Recurring General Journals

Used for repeating expenses (Rent, Subscriptions). You must define a Recurring Method:

  • Fixed: The amount remains the same every time.
  • Variable: The amount is zeroed out after posting (you must type a new amount next month).
  • Balance: Allocates the entire balance of an account (e.g., clear out a petty cash account to zero).

Standard Journals

A template feature. You can save a journal with lines (e.g., Payroll split) and reload it next month to save typing.

3. Bank Reconciliation

Matching your internal Bank Ledger Entries (BLE) to the actual Bank Statement.

Matching Methods

  • Automatic Matching: BC tries to match based on Date, Amount, and Transaction Text. You can configure "Transaction Text Matching" rules to improve accuracy.
  • Manual Matching: Select a line on the left (Statement) and right (BLE) and click "Match Manually".

Transfer to General Journal

If you find a bank fee on the statement that you forgot to record in BC, you can click "Transfer to General Journal" right from the Recon screen to create and post it immediately.
