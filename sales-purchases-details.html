1. Inventory Costing Methods

You cannot change the costing method of an item once transactions exist. Choose carefully.

Methods Defined

  • FIFO (First-In, First-Out): Standard for distribution. The cost of goods sold is taken from the oldest inventory. The Balance Sheet value reflects the most recent costs.
  • LIFO (Last-In, First-Out): Cost of goods sold is taken from the newest inventory.
  • Average: A weighted average. You can configure "Average Cost Period" (Day, Week, Month) and "Average Cost Calc. Type" (Item, or Item & Location & Variant).
  • Specific: Used for high-value items (cars, art). You must identify exactly which serial number was sold, and BC uses the exact cost of that specific serial number.
  • Standard: Used in Manufacturing. The cost is "Frozen". Any difference between purchase price and standard cost is posted immediately to a Purchase Variance Account.

2. Sales Price Lists

The "New Sales Pricing Experience" consolidates prices and discounts into "Price Lists".

Setup Options

  • Assign-to Type: Who gets this price? (All Customers, Customer, Customer Price Group, Campaign).
  • Asset Type: What are we selling? (Item, G/L Account, Resource, Service Cost).
  • Minimum Quantity: Price only triggers if they buy 10+.
  • Unit of Measure: Price is $10 for "PCS" but $80 for "BOX" (of 10).
Best Price Logic: If a customer qualifies for two prices (e.g., they are in the "Retail" group which gets $10, but they have a specific customer price of $9), Business Central always gives the lowest price.

3. Item Tracking (Serials & Lots)

Configured via Item Tracking Codes.

Key Settings

  • SN Specific Tracking: If true, you cannot move/sell the item without selecting a Serial No.
  • Lot Specific Tracking: Same as above, but for Batches/Lots.
  • Expiration Date Formula: Requires user to enter expiry date on inbound (Purchase). Enforces "FEFO" (First Expired, First Out) picking.
